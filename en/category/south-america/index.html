My top 10 of South America

It was very difficult to prepare a top 10 list for South America. Each country even each city has its charm and beauty. It would have been much easier to prepare a top 20 but I took up the challenge. I hope this list helps people while choosing their destinations when they don’t have much time to visit the whole South America.

1. Galapagos  Islands

Let’s follow the footsteps of Charles Darwin and enjoy nature, sea, fishes, sea lions, giant tortoises and varieties of birds.

2. Salar de Uyuni

3 days were definitely not enough. Not just the Salar but also all the lakes around, colourful mountains, the desert of Dali, the cemetery of trains. All of it is amazing.

3. Waterfalls of Iguazu

You can enjoy them closely from Argentina and have a wonderful panoramic view from Brazil. They are so big, that you will feel pretty small.

4. Perito Moreno

Even though I heard before going that it is immense, I couldn’t imagine the size until I saw it with my own eyes. They rise 70 meters above the sea level.  You will be fascinated.

5. Trekking of Santa Cruz

Trekking lovers will have 4 days without all kind of technology and experience extreme changes of climate. You can fully enjoy mountains during the day and the stars during the night.

6. Torres del Paine

Another awesome trek in the Chilean Patagonia with awesome views. Unfortunately some parts of the park are burned down but still the beauty remained.

7. Cuzco and Machu Pichhu

In Cuzco you can easily picture yourself in the past wandering through houses and streets built with Inka stones. And Machu Picchu…it doesn’t matter how many photos you have seen of it, you will still get shocked and amazed.

8. Trekking of Ciudad Perdida

You will dance with mud, dance with rain and dance with rivers all throughout the jungle until you reach your well-deserved destination: The Lost City.

9. Rio de Janeiro

Gorgeous kilometric beaches with mountains surrounding them, forest in the background, favelas and lovely, friendly Brazilians.

10. Cartagena de Indias

Colourful colonial buildings, stylish restaurants and cafes, salsa and joyful people.
